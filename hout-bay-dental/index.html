Chapter One · The Story

A practice that grew up with the bay.

Inside · Joslyn Place

In 1983 there was one chair, one dentist, and a doorway onto Victoria Avenue. Dr Andy Effting had graduated top of his class at Wits two years before, and he wanted to do something specific: dentistry that respected the body, used the smallest materials possible, and didn't rely on mercury.

That was unusual then. It still is. Forty-two years later the practice has four full-time dentists, three operatories, and patients who arrive from Cape Town, Amsterdam, and München in the same week — but the principles haven't moved: keep it conservative, keep it beautiful, treat the person rather than the tooth.

The work runs the full range. Cleanings and check-ups for kids who have been coming here since their first molars. Veneers and Digital Smile Design for the wedding next month. Implants planned with CBCT scans, milled on a CEREC the same afternoon. Bruxism appliances for the grinders. And, quietly, a lot of the holistic, mercury-free, minimally invasive work that started this all in the eighties.

It's one thing just doing teeth and making them work. It's another thing to also create beauty and balance, and improve comfort. — Dr Jean van Lierop

Chapter Three · The Founder

Dr Andy Effting

A. Effting

Top of class, Wits 1981.

Two years later he opened a single-chair practice on Victoria Avenue and decided to do something nobody much else was doing in Hout Bay yet: mercury-free, minimally invasive, aesthetic dentistry. He's still in the practice every week.

Along the way he co-founded the South African Academy of Aesthetic Dentistry, served as its president, became a Fellow of the International Team for Implantology, and started his own at-home oral care line. The practice is now four dentists strong, but the philosophy hasn't changed.
